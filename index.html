I'm Gabriela Barreto Lemos (she/her/hers), a quantum physics Professor at the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro in Brazil. I'm also engaged in creative interdisciplinary projects and promoting inclusion and diversity in physics.

Quantum Physics Research

My research focus is quantum optics, with emphasis on quantum foundations, quantum imaging and quantum information. If you would like to know more about my research, you can read my papers for free on the arXiv.

Interdisciplinary Projects

The Fabric of Space-Time

What is your preferred fabric of space-time? In reference to the commodification of scientific projects, Kayla Lewis and I pose as personal stylists, connecting each participant to fabrics and designs that represent the main features of three quantum space-time hypotheses competing today for funding and recognition.

Decoherence 1.0

A continual feedback between light and sound, science and art. With Irradiation Reloaded and Prof. Mehul Malik.

Yes or No?

Maybe yes and no? This survey is designed to capture that the pauses and intonations that reveal the hidden ambiguities behind seemingly binary answers to all sorts of questions we pose to the participant. With Kayla Lewis.

Quantum Photography

The famous image from our quantum imaging method was invited to be part of the ArBio Brasil exhibit of scientific art. With Tori Borish, Radek Lapkiewicz, Sven Ramelow, Garett Cole and Anton Zeilinger.

Somnium

A powerful story of a witch-hunt at the heart of the history of physics. Illustrations by Jacques Pienaar.

Nostalgia for the light

A course interweaving physics, cosmology, culture and social issues, inspired by Patrizio Guzmán's magnificent documentary "Nostalgia for the Light". Honors students at the University of Massachusetts Boston show off their talent and creativity in their final course works inspired by complex scientific concepts.

Women in physics

Little seeds of science

A project initiated with Laura Corredor Bohórquez to encourage and inspire young girls to remain curious and enjoy learning science
